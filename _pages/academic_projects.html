A brief look at some of the work I did during my four years of University. As these were mostly for assignments, I will not provide code publicly.

Texture Generation

C++, SDL, OpenGL

Small piece of software done as a research project that generates textures from applying frequency-domain filters to a noisy image.

Rush Hour Solver

Python, Z3

Solver for the game 'Rush Hour,' a sliding puzzle consisting of different sized 'cars' that move vertically and horizontally, where the goal is to get one specific car across the board to the exit.

View

3D Breakout

C++, FreeGLUT, OpenGL

The game of Breakout upgraded to 3D, including a few Nintendo-themed levels.

View

Object recognition

Matlab

Matlab program that recognises known objects (food labels in this case) in a video by matching SURF keypoints.

Other projects include a Lua parser written in Python, a Lego Mindstorms robot programmed with LeJOS to navigate a grid with paths blocked by obstacles, an investigation into predicting the outcomes of DotA 2 matches, and a client-server software suite to detect fraudulent data in a (made-up) stream of stock market transactions, as part of a group software project in my 2nd year.
